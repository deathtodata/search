About

Death2Data provides private search infrastructure. Like Signal for messaging, we separate identity from content.

The Principle: We know who you are. We don't know what you search.

This service is built to internet infrastructure standards. No tracking scripts. No analytics. No third-party requests. View source to verify.

Why We Log Logins

Like Signal's phone number requirement, login records provide:

  • Abuse prevention (rate limiting by token)
  • Legal compliance (can identify account, not activity)
  • Security (detect unauthorized token use)

We cannot associate any search query with any user. By design.

Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2025-01-24

What We Collect

When you create an account: Nothing. We don't have accounts.

When you purchase access: Stripe collects payment information. We receive: confirmation of payment, nothing else. We generate a token and give it to you. We store only the SHA-256 hash of this token.

When you log in: We record the token hash, your IP address, your browser's user agent string, and the timestamp.

When you search: Nothing. We do not log, store, or process search queries. We cannot.

What We Don't Collect

  • Search queries
  • Search results
  • Browsing history
  • Cookies (we don't use them)
  • Email addresses (Stripe has yours, we don't)
  • Names
  • Any form of user profile

Third Parties

Stripe: Processes payments. Their privacy policy applies to payment data.

Search engines: SearXNG queries Google, Bing, etc. on your behalf. They see our server's IP, not yours. They cannot associate queries with you.

No one else: We don't use analytics, advertising, social media integrations, or any other third-party services.

Data Retention

Your Rights

Request your data: We can provide your token hash and associated login records. We cannot provide search history because it doesn't exist.

Delete your data: Email privacy@death2data.com with your token. We'll delete the hash and all login records.

Law Enforcement

If we receive a valid legal request, we can provide:

  • Token hash (not the token itself)
  • Login timestamps, IPs, and user agents

We cannot provide search queries because we don't have them. We will publish a transparency report annually.
